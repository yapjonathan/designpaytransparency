Establishing a "thought leader" presence
One of the interesting things that came out of the internet age is the decentralization and democratization of content creation. Once upon a time, there were only so many channels of mass communication with an inherent "gatekeeping" process. For example, to publish a book, you would need to work with a publisher. To produce a video, you would need way to distribute it.
Now, anyone with an internet connection and a phone can publish blogs, articles, videos, podcasts, etc. With the popularity of so many content platforms like youtube, spotify, instagram, etc., the barriers to entry are lower than ever.
That being said, it still comes down to that tri-fecta of luck, privilege and hard work to build an audience and take content creation to the next level (my next level I mean monetizing it). Few people successfully take a fun side hobby and start making a decent living off of it.
Before we get started... Disclaimer: I do not really consider myself a "content creator" so please take my opinions with a grain of salt.
Will being a content creator help me land a job?
My two cents is that the majority of content creation streams are not ideal for advancing immediate goals like “get a job”. If you go into it with that mindset, it is easy to get discouraged and give up when the return on your efforts feel negligible.
Being a successful content creator tends to be a long-term goal. Most “successful” influencers spent years in obscurity, religiously producing content to a handful of loyal fans until they caught their big break. I feel that unless you truly enjoy the content creation process, it is extremely hard to stick at it long enough to catch that “break”.
This is contrasted with "getting a job" which is a short term goal. Most people's timeline for getting a job is "as soon as possible" so this make content-creation a rather inefficient way to achieve that goal. If your goal is to “get a job” then your focus on your portfolio and networking.

If you also happen to enjoy creating content and want to share your journey to share with others.. or feel that you have some unique points of views to add (bonus points if it is somewhat niche and stems from your experiences)... then you should definitely go for it! This is a classic case of enjoying the journey and not just the destination.
There is definitely potential for huge success
If you can get the ball rolling on any of these “thought leader” areas the payout is huge! Once you gain enough followers or have a large enough community, you bet that it will be an incredible way to "stand out" from the crowd. For example, if you have a YouTube channel on UX with 250k subs, then employers will notice.
A big point I want to call out is that the content creation field is pretty saturated and the consumers of the content are growing pickier with each passing year. In my opinion, it can be quite obvious if someone seems to be producing content just for clout and as a result, the end product lacks that passion that builds a community of loyal fans and supporters.
But if despite all this, you still feel passionate about creating content then I have nothing but encouragement for you! Thank you for contributing to the wonderfully robust UX community <3

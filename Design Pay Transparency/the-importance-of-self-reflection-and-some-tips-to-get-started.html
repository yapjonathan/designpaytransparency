The importance of self-reflection and some tips to get started
1 backlink
Human self-reflection is the capacity of humans to exercise introspection and to attempt to learn more about their fundamental nature and essence.
Self reflection is a powerful way of combing through your thoughts and actions to find patterns that can help you both come to terms with your past/current situation AND prepare for future scenarios. I won't go into details on the details but I recommend this Medium article from an expert in Cognitive Science if you want to dive deeper into this subject.
One of the best ways to get started is to commit to a practice that will help you achieve a desired outcome over time. It really helps to have a routine where you set aside some time on a recurring basis. For example, this could be 15 minutes every 3 days.
When you reflect, do it with focused intent. In this case when you might be undertaking this process for the first time or resetting anew, start by asking yourself what you want and translate it into a goal.
It might be clear or it might not be. Start by asking yourself how you want to feel instead if it isn’t clear.
Here are some questions you can try asking yourself about this goal:
•
What will it take to reach it?
•
What kind of changes do you need to make about yourself?
•
What habits are positive and which are negative?
•
What changes to you need to make to your surroundings, including who you surround yourself with?
Some questions might be easy, some might be super difficult. The most important thing is to be honest with yourself. When you have your answers, write it all down as clearly and as detailed as possible. It becomes more real this way. Using your answers as a guideline, turn them into actionable items and tie times to them.
Here is an example:
Issue: I feel burned out because it feels like I am always working and I have no time to myself
Goal: I want to take control of my free time after work and pursue activities that make me happy
Actionable item: Disconnect from Slack and email after work.
Next you want to keep yourself accountable to those changes you wish to make. Keep yourself honest or better yet find someone to hold you accountable. When your focus is on yourself and self-improvement, you'll begin to frame a lot of your behaviours on the job in a similar way and with greater conviction.
Never too late to get started
The most effective way to stay committed to something is to cultivate it as a habit. Imagine real-time self-reflection as a mental muscle that with the right training, can be deployed at a moment's notice to help you understand your thoughts and direct your actions.
Why not get started today?
